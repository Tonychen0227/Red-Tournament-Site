Format

This year, we'll be using running a new format called Ascension Swiss. Unlike previous years, there will not be a one-round seeding round.

Instead, there will be a Group Stage of three races for all competitors. The first round of the group stage will see matches of 2-3 racers formed completely randomly, minimizing the number of 2-player races.

After the first round, all the winners will make their way to the Ascension Pool, while the remaining racers remain in the Normal Pool.

For the remaining two rounds of the Group Stage, players will be placed in races drawn from players in their respective Pool (Ascension/Normal). Winners in the Ascension Pool will advance straight to the Quarter-Finals, while winning a race in the Normal Pool will grant advancement to the next round's Ascension Pool.

In addition to Pool Advancements, all racers will earn points based on their performance during each round in the Group Stage, as shown later.

After the Group Stage, the top 27 players will compete in a single-elimination winner-takes-all bracket consisting of the Quarter-Finals, Semi-Finals, and Finals.

Group Stage

Each round in the Group Stage will see all competitors be matched in a 3-player race with other competitors from their same bracket. There may be some 2-player races possible due to bracket imbalances, but will be minimized where possible.

Everyone begins in the Normal Pool for the first round, with the following point distributions:

  • Winner: 10 points and advancement to Ascension Pool
  • Middle*: 6 points
  • Bottom: 0 points

* Only applies to 2nd place in 3-player races. Coming in 2nd in a 2-player race is considered bottom position

Tiebreakers

In general, tiebreakers will not be resolved, and all players with the same point totals will share positions.

The exception is when the number of players with equal or more points than the racer in 27th position exceeds 27. In this case, every player with the same points total as the 27th position will play a tiebreaker match to determine advancement to the Quarter-Finals

The top X players from the tiebreaker match will advance to the Quarter-Finals, where X = 27 - K, where K is the number of players with more points than the number of points in shared 27th position.

For example, if 25 players have over 20 points, and the 26-29 position players all have 18 points, the tiebreaker match will consist of four races with two advancing to the Quarter-Finals.

Top Cut

After the Swiss rounds, the top 27 players will advance to nine (9) Quarter-Finals matches, with the winners moving on to three (3) Semi-Finals matches. Winners of the Semi-Finals advance to the Finals.

For the Quarter-Finals, players will be ordered and then placed into three pots in order. Earning advancement in Round 2 earns the highest seed, followed by earning a Bye in Round 3, followed by highest point total. Ties for seeding go in favor of the runner with the better tournament personal best. Each match will consist of a player from each pot.
